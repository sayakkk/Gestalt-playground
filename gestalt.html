01 / LAW OF

Proximity

Nähe

Elements placed near one another are perceived as belonging together. Distance speaks louder than appearance — identical dots become rows, columns, or clusters depending purely on their spacing.

The space between objects is never empty. It is the argument for what belongs with what.

→ Switch between four modes. See the same dots regroup.

02 / LAW OF

Similarity

Ähnlichkeit

Objects that share visual properties — shape, color, size — are grouped together, even when spatially separated. As long as a property "varies together" across a subset, that subset reads as a group.

Switch the axis and the groups reorganize. By shape, by color, by size — each axis carves up the same field differently.

→ Try each axis. Same positions, different groupings.
Axis
03 / LAW OF

Continuity

Fortsetzung

The eye prefers to follow the smoothest path. When two strokes meet, we read them as continuous flows, not as broken segments.

A single curve is split at the midpoint into two halves, each rotated to a random angle. Use the slider to rotate them simultaneously and find the angle that restores the original continuous line.

→ When the two halves snap into one flow, the unified curve briefly reveals itself.

04 / LAW OF

Closure

Geschlossenheit

The mind completes what the eye cannot. Given fragmentary information, perception supplies the missing contour and delivers a whole, closed form.

The Kanizsa figure makes lines that don't exist visible. When the pac-man notches find their alignment, a triangle, a cube, a star rises out of empty space.

→ Each shape starts at a random angle. Slide to find the moment of alignment.

05 / LAW OF

Figure & Ground

Figur-Grund

Perception divides every scene into a figure (what you attend to) and a ground (everything else). The split is not in the image. It is in you.

Cues that decide what counts as figure — convexity, layering, size — change the reading entirely. Same image, different interpretation.

→ Switch between three cues and use Invert to flip what reads as figure.
Cue
06 / LAW OF

Common Fate

Gemeinsames Schicksal

Elements that move together are perceived as a unit — even when they differ in every other respect. Shared motion is the strongest grouping cue of all.

Draw a region over the dots to form a group, then drag inside the region to set its direction. At rest, the dots are scattered. The moment they move, the groups appear.

→ ① Drag in empty space to lasso a group → ② Drag inside the region to set direction → ③ Press Play.
